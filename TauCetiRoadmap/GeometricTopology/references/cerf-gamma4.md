# Fair-use extract: Cerf, Γ₄ = 0: diffeomorphisms of S³

**Source.** J. Cerf, *Sur les difféomorphismes de la sphère de dimension trois (Γ₄ = 0)*, Springer Lecture Notes in Mathematics 53, 1968.

**Locus.** Chapitre I, §1 ("Définition du groupe Γₙ ; énoncé du théorème"). Théorème 1, Corollaire 1 (Γ₄ = 0), and Corollaire 2 are on printed p. 3 (physical djvu p. 17). The definition of Γₙ as the cokernel of αₙ and the supporting lemmas are on printed pp. 1–2 (physical djvu pp. 15–16). Offset: printed page = djvu page − 14.

**Supports.** GeometricTopology roadmap, layer 3 (diffeomorphism groups, π₀ Diff(S³)).

*(Short attributed quotations of the essential statement (French) with an English summary, surrounding material summarised, for scholarly reference.)*

## Summary (English)

Cerf defines Diff Sⁿ (resp. Diff Dⁿ⁺¹) as the group of orientation-preserving diffeomorphisms of Sⁿ (resp. Dⁿ⁺¹), with the C^∞ topology (printed p. 1). The canonical map αₙ : Diff Dⁿ⁺¹ → Diff Sⁿ (restriction to the boundary) has, by Lemmas 1 and 2, an image that is a normal subgroup of Diff Sⁿ with abelian cokernel; *by definition* that cokernel is the group Γₙ₊₁ (printed p. 2). By a result attributed to Munkres, Γₙ₊₁ is canonically isomorphic to the cokernel of π₀(Diff Dⁿ⁺¹) → π₀(Diff Sⁿ) (printed pp. 2–3).

The main result is:

- **Théorème 1** (printed p. 3): the group π₀(Diff S³) is trivial. (My translation: the group of orientation-preserving diffeomorphisms of S³ is connected, i.e. every such diffeomorphism is isotopic to the identity.)
- **Corollaire 1** (printed p. 3): consequently Γ₄ = 0. (My translation, unpacking the definition above: every orientation-preserving diffeomorphism of S³ extends to a diffeomorphism of the 4-ball D⁴; equivalently the canonical map Diff D⁴ → Diff S³ is surjective on path-components, so the cokernel Γ₄ is trivial.)
- **Corollaire 2** (printed p. 3): the group π₃ (the group of homeomorphisms of S³ onto S³, in context the mapping-class-group sense used here) is canonically isomorphic to π₃(SO(4)). (My translation; this follows from Théorème 1 combined with reference [3].)

In §2 (printed p. 3) Cerf restates Théorème 1, via Proposition 4 of the Appendix, as the equivalent assertion π₀(Diff(D³ ; S²)) = 0, where Diff(D³ ; S²) is the group of diffeomorphisms of D³ inducing the identity on S² = ∂D³.

## Key statement (quoted, French)

From printed p. 3 (djvu p. 17), opening of §1's conclusion:

> Ceci posé, notre but est de démontrer le théorème suivant :
>
> **Théorème 1.** Le groupe π₀(Diff S³) est nul.
>
> Il résulte de la conséquence 2° ci-dessus que le théorème 1 a le corollaire suivant :
>
> **Corollaire 1.** Le groupe Γ₄ est nul.
>
> Dans un autre ordre d'idées, le théorème 1, compte tenu de [3], a la conséquence suivante :
>
> **Corollaire 2.** Le groupe π₃ (groupe des homéomorphismes de S³ sur S³) est canoniquement isomorphe à π₃(SO(4)).

The defining property of Γₙ₊₁, from printed p. 2 (djvu p. 16):

> Lemme 2. L'image de l'application canonique αₙ : Diff Dⁿ⁺¹ → Diff Sⁿ contient la composante connexe de l'élément neutre dans Diff Sⁿ.

> Conséquences des lemmes 1 et 2.
> 1° L'image de l'application αₙ est un sous-groupe distingué de Diff Dⁿ, et le conoyau de αₙ est un groupe abélien.
> Par définition, le conoyau de αₙ est le groupe Γₙ₊₁.

(My translation of the last lines: "Consequences of Lemmas 1 and 2. 1° The image of the map αₙ is a normal subgroup of Diff Dⁿ, and the cokernel of αₙ is an abelian group. By definition, the cokernel of αₙ is the group Γₙ₊₁.")

The equivalent reformulation, from printed p. 3 (djvu p. 17), §2:

> D'après la proposition 4 de l'Appendice, le théorème 1 équivaut à
>
> (2)  π₀(Diff(D³ ; S²)) = 0
>
> (où Diff(D³ ; S²) désigne le groupe des difféomorphismes de D³ qui induisent l'identité sur S²).

## Notes

- The cover/title page renders "Γ₄ = O" (OCR sometimes shows "r4 = O"); the printed mathematical text uses Γ throughout. The title's "(Γ₄ = 0)" matches Corollaire 1.
- Subscript reading corrections from the rendered pages: the title and Chapitre I heading concern **Γ₄** (not "T4" as the rough OCR suggested, the OCR renders the capital Γ as "r"/"T"). Throughout §1 the group is **Γₙ** and the corollary group is **Γ₄**.
- "Le groupe π₃" in Corollaire 2: the printed subscript is 3; in context it denotes the group of (orientation-preserving) homeomorphisms / the relevant mapping object of S³, as Cerf parenthesises ("groupe des homéomorphismes de S³ sur S³"). Quoted verbatim above; the parenthetical is Cerf's own gloss.
- The C^∞ topology is used for the diffeomorphism groups (printed p. 1).
- N. H. Kuiper's English summary at the front of the volume (printed p. 1 of the summary, djvu p. 11) independently states the headline result: "any two differential structures on the topological 4-sphere, both obtainable by gluing two 4-discs along their boundaries, are diffeomorphic (Γ₄ = 0)", proved via π₀(Diff S³) = π₀(Diff D⁴) = 1 (OCR-rendered; quoted here only as corroboration, not as the primary statement).
