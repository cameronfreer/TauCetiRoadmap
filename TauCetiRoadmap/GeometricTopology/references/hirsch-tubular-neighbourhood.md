# Fair-use extract: Hirsch, Differential Topology: tubular neighbourhood existence and isotopy uniqueness
**Source.** M. Hirsch, Differential Topology, Springer GTM 33, 1976.
**Locus.** Chapter 4 §6 ("Collars and Tubular Neighborhoods of Neat Submanifolds"): existence Theorem 6.3 (printed p. 114), closed-tubular isotopy Theorem 6.5 (printed p. 117); and Chapter 4 §5 isotopy-uniqueness Theorem 5.3 (printed p. 112). Physical djvu pp. 62–64.
**Supports.** GeometricTopology roadmap, layer 1 (existence and essential uniqueness, up to ambient isotopy, of tubular neighbourhoods of neat submanifolds).
*(Short attributed quotations of the essential statements, surrounding material summarised, for scholarly reference.)*

## Summary
Existence: Theorem 6.3 states that a neat submanifold M ⊂ V has a tubular neighborhood in V. The proof uses the collar refinement (Theorem 6.2) to handle the boundary, reduces a special case V = W × I, and embeds ∂V in a Euclidean half-space to build a normal tubular neighborhood.

Uniqueness up to isotopy: Theorem 5.3 (in §5, for the boundaryless case ∂M = ∂V = ∅) states that any two tubular neighborhoods of M in V are isotopic. The proof constructs the canonical homotopy from the fibre derivative Φ to g = f₁⁻¹f₀ and checks it is C∞ via Taylor's formula.

Closed tubular neighborhoods: a *closed tubular neighborhood of radius ε > 0* is the restriction to the disk subbundle Dₑ(ξ) = {x ∈ E : |x| ≤ ε} of a tubular neighborhood. Theorem 6.5 is the isotopy theorem for these, producing an isotopy of tubular neighborhoods carrying one closed disk bundle onto another. Theorem 6.4 is an extension theorem (every tubular neighborhood of ∂M in ∂V extends).

## Key statements (quoted)
Isotopy uniqueness (§5, p. 112):

> "**5.3. Theorem.** Let M ⊂ V be a submanifold, ∂M = ∂V = ∅. Then any two tubular neighborhoods of M in V are isotopic." — Hirsch, Theorem 5.3, p. 112.

Existence (§6, p. 114):

> "**6.3. Theorem.** Let M ⊂ V be a neat submanifold. Then M has a tubular neighborhood in V." — Hirsch, Theorem 6.3, p. 114.

Extension theorem (p. 115):

> "**6.4. Theorem.** Let M ⊂ V be a neat submanifold. Then every tubular neighborhood of ∂M in ∂V is the intersection with ∂V of a tubular neighborhood for M in V." — Hirsch, Theorem 6.4, p. 115.

Definition of a closed tubular neighborhood (p. 117):

> "A closed tubular neighborhood of radius ε > 0, of a submanifold M ⊂ V, is an embedding Dₑ(ξ) → M which is the restriction of a tubular neighborhood (f,ξ = (p,E,M)) of M. Here
>
>     Dₑ(ξ) = {x ∈ E : |x| ≤ ε}
>
> is the disk subbundle of ξ of radius ε, for a given orthogonal structure on ξ." — Hirsch, p. 117.

Isotopy theorem for closed tubular neighborhoods (p. 117):

> "**6.5. Theorem.** Let M ⊂ V be a submanifold. Let ξᵢ = (pᵢ,Eᵢ,M) be orthogonal vector bundles over M, i = 0, 1. Let (fᵢ,ξᵢ) be a tubular neighborhood of M. Let ε > 0, δ > 0. Then (f₀,ξ₀) and (f₁,ξ₁) are isotopic by an isotopy of tubular neighborhoods Fₜ: E → V, 0 ≤ t ≤ 1, such that F₀ = f₀ and F₁(Dₑ(ξ₀)) = D_δ(ξ₁)." — Hirsch, Theorem 6.5, p. 117.
