# Fair-use extract: Hirsch, Differential Topology: disc theorem (embeddings with positive Jacobian are isotopic)
**Source.** M. Hirsch, Differential Topology, Springer GTM 33, 1976.
**Locus.** Theorem 6.6, Chapter 4 §6 ("Collars and Tubular Neighborhoods of Neat Submanifolds"), printed p. 117; physical djvu p. 64.
**Supports.** GeometricTopology roadmap, layer 1 (the form of Palais's disc theorem used to prove that connected sum is well defined: two embeddings of Dⁿ differing by an orientation-preserving germ at 0 are ambiently isotopic).
*(Short attributed quotations of the essential statements, surrounding material summarised, for scholarly reference.)*

## Summary
Hirsch sets up the case where the submanifold is a single point x₀ ∈ V: an *open tubular neighborhood* of x₀ is an embedding (ℝⁿ,0) → (V,x₀), and a *closed tubular neighborhood of radius 1* is an embedding (Dⁿ,0) → (V,x₀), where n = dim V (with ∂V = ∅ assumed). Theorem 6.6 then says that two such embeddings of Eⁿ (= Dⁿ or ℝⁿ) into V agreeing in orientation at 0, i.e. with positive Jacobian determinant of f₁⁻¹f₀ at 0, are isotopic rel 0. The proof reduces (via isotopy of tubular neighborhoods) to the case where f₁⁻¹f₀ is a linear automorphism L ∈ GL(n); when det L > 0, L lies in the identity component of GL(n), giving an arc Lₜ from L to the identity and hence the required isotopy.

## Key statements (quoted)
Set-up for the point case (p. 117):

> "As a very special but useful case, let M be a point x₀ ∈ V. An open tubular neighborhood of x₀ is an embedding (ℝⁿ,0) → (V,x₀) and a closed tubular neighborhood of radius 1 is an embedding (Dⁿ,0) → (V,x₀), where n = dim V. (We suppose ∂V = ∅.)" — Hirsch, p. 117.

The theorem (p. 117):

> "**6.6. Theorem.** Let Eⁿ = Dⁿ or ℝⁿ and let fᵢ:(Eⁿ,0) → (V,x₀) be an embedding, i = 0, 1, where n = dim V. If
>
>     Det(D(f₁⁻¹f₀)(0)) > 0
>
> then f₀ and f₁ are isotopic rel 0." — Hirsch, Theorem 6.6, p. 117.
